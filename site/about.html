◆ About This Project

What is .bank.in? In April 2025, the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) mandated that all Indian banks migrate to .bank.in domains by October 31, 2025, to reduce phishing and spoofing attacks. The registry is operated by the Institute for Development and Research in Banking Technology (IDRBT), with the technical portal built by IKCON Technologies.

Who runs this? This scorecard is an independent project by CashlessConsumer. It has no affiliation with IDRBT, RBI, NIXI, or any government agency. It is a public transparency initiative — because the regulator does not publish security benchmarks for its own TLD.

Data sources:

  • IDRBT portal billing endpoint — 1,535 invoices extracted, yielding 1,497 unique registered .bank.in domains
  • crt.sh Certificate Transparency logs — 6,543 certificate entries triangulated against the domain list
  • IFSC codes & DICGC deposit insurance data — used to cross-reference bank names, types, and categories

Methodology note: Automated OSINT scans from a single vantage point. Results are based on externally observable signals and may differ from a bank's internal security posture. A passing score does not guarantee security — a failing score is a strong indicator of risk.

20 pts

📡 HTTP Reachability

10 pts

5 pts — Returns 200, 301, or 302 status

★ Grade Boundaries

A 80 – 100 Excellent — bank-grade security posture. Minimal attack surface.
B 60 – 79 Good — minor gaps. Most security controls in place.
C 40 – 59 Fair — several gaps present. Significant improvement needed.
D 20 – 39 Poor — critical gaps. High risk of compromise.
F 0 – 19 Failing — fundamentally insecure. Immediate action required.

⚠ Limitations

◆ Single vantage point: All scans originate from one AWS region. Results may differ from other geographic perspectives or internal network positions.
◆ DNS variability: DNS checks reflect the resolver configuration at scan time. Banks using split-horizon or geo-aware DNS may show different results internally.
◆ No authenticated scans: We do not log in, submit forms, or interact with applications. Login portals, internal APIs, and authenticated areas are not tested.
◆ Static analysis only: No dynamic testing (XSS, SQLi, CSRF, path traversal). A high score does not mean the application is secure — only that basic transport and config hygiene is in place.
◆ Not a substitute for professional audit: This is a public OSINT transparency tool, not a penetration test or security audit. Banks should engage qualified professionals for comprehensive assessments.
